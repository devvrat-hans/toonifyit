Introduction

This Cookie Policy explains how Toonifyit.com ("we," "our," "us") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies when you visit our website at https://toonifyit.com (the "Website").

This policy should be read in conjunction with our Privacy Policy. By continuing to use our Website, you consent to our use of cookies as described in this policy.

Important: Your converter data (JSON/TOON inputs) is processed entirely in your browser and is never transmitted to our servers. Cookies are used only for website functionality, analytics, and advertising purposes.

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that websites store on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile) when you visit them. They serve several purposes:

  • Session Management: Remember your preferences and settings
  • Analytics: Help us understand how you use our website
  • Advertising: Display relevant ads and measure their effectiveness
  • Security: Detect fraud and abuse

Cookies can be "persistent" (remain on your device until expiration or deletion) or "session" (deleted when you close your browser).

Similar Technologies: We also use local storage, session storage, and pixels/beacons, which function similarly to cookies but store data differently.

Types of Cookies We Use

We categorize cookies into four main types based on their purpose:

1. Essential Cookies (Strictly Necessary)

These cookies are required for the website to function properly and cannot be disabled without breaking core functionality.

What we use them for:

  • Theme preference (dark/light mode toggle)
  • Conversion history storage (last 5 conversions, local only)
  • Delimiter and formatting options
  • Session management and security

Legal Basis: Legitimate interest (necessary for website operation)

Storage Method: Browser localStorage and sessionStorage

Expiration: Persistent (remains until you clear browser data)

2. Analytics Cookies (Performance)

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting anonymous usage data.

Service Provider: Google Analytics

What we collect:

  • Pages visited and time spent on each page
  • Click patterns and navigation paths
  • Browser type, operating system, device type
  • Referral source (how you found our website)
  • Approximate geographic location (city/region level)

Legal Basis: Consent (you can opt out via browser settings or cookie banner)

Data Retention: 26 months (Google Analytics default)

Opt-Out: Use Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on

3. Advertising Cookies (Targeting/Marketing)

These cookies are used to display relevant ads and measure ad campaign effectiveness.

Service Provider: Google AdSense (Google LLC)

What they do:

  • Show personalized ads based on your browsing history
  • Limit the number of times you see the same ad (frequency capping)
  • Measure ad clicks and conversions
  • Build advertising profiles (interest-based advertising)

Third-Party Partners: Google may share cookie data with its advertising partners (DoubleClick, YouTube, etc.)

Legal Basis: Consent (required under GDPR and CCPA)

Opt-Out Options:

  • Google Ads Settings - Manage personalized ads
  • NAI Opt-Out Tool - Opt out of multiple ad networks
  • DAA Consumer Choice Page - Manage ad preferences

4. Third-Party Cookies

Cookies set by external services we integrate with (not directly controlled by us):

  • Google Analytics: _ga, _gid, _gat (analytics tracking)
  • Google AdSense: IDE, DSID, __gads (advertising)
  • DoubleClick: test_cookie, NID (ad serving)

These third parties have their own privacy policies:

  • Google Privacy Policy
  • Google Advertising Policies

How to Manage Cookies

You have the right to accept or reject cookies. Here are your options:

Browser Cookie Settings

All modern browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. Here's how to manage cookies in popular browsers:

Google Chrome

  1. Open Chrome Settings (⚙️ icon or chrome://settings/)
  2. Navigate to Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data
  3. Choose: Block all cookies, Block third-party cookies, or Allow all cookies
  4. To delete existing cookies: See all site data and permissions → Remove

Mozilla Firefox

  1. Open Firefox Settings (☰ menu → Settings)
  2. Go to Privacy & Security
  3. Under Cookies and Site Data, click Manage Data
  4. Select cookies to remove or use Clear Data

Safari (macOS/iOS)

  1. Open Safari Preferences (⚙️ → Preferences)
  2. Go to Privacy tab
  3. Check Block all cookies or Prevent cross-site tracking
  4. To delete: Manage Website Data → Remove

Microsoft Edge

  1. Open Edge Settings (⋯ menu → Settings)
  2. Navigate to Cookies and site permissions → Cookies and data stored
  3. Choose cookie preferences (Allow, Block third-party, Block all)
  4. To delete: See all cookies and site data → Remove

⚠️ Warning: Blocking all cookies may break website functionality (e.g., dark mode toggle, conversion history).

Cookie Consent Banner

When you first visit our website, you may see a cookie consent banner. You can:

  • Accept All: Allows all cookies (essential, analytics, advertising)
  • Reject Non-Essential: Only essential cookies are enabled
  • Customize: Choose which cookie categories to enable

You can change your preferences anytime by clearing cookies or revisiting the consent banner (look for a "Cookie Settings" link in our footer).

COPPA Compliance

Local Storage & Session Storage

In addition to cookies, we use browser storage APIs:

Local Storage (Persistent)

Stores data with no expiration time. Used for:

  • theme - Dark/light mode preference
  • conversionHistory - Last 5 JSON ↔ TOON conversions (never transmitted to server)
  • delimiterPreference - Chosen delimiter (comma, tab, pipe)

Session Storage (Temporary)

Stores data for the duration of your browsing session. Used for:

  • Temporary state management
  • Form inputs (not submitted)

Clearing Storage Data

To clear local/session storage:

  • Chrome/Edge: DevTools (F12) → Application → Local Storage → Delete
  • Firefox: DevTools (F12) → Storage → Local Storage → Delete
  • Safari: Develop menu → Show Web Inspector → Storage

Do Not Track (DNT) Signals

Some browsers support "Do Not Track" (DNT) signals, which request that websites not track you.

Our Approach:

  • We respect DNT signals for analytics cookies (Google Analytics will honor DNT)
  • Advertising cookies (Google AdSense) may still be set, but you can opt out separately via Google Ads Settings
  • Essential cookies remain active (required for website functionality)

How to Enable DNT

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Send a "Do Not Track" request
  • Firefox: Settings → Privacy & Security → Send websites a "Do Not Track" signal
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Prevent cross-site tracking
